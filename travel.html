Travel to Amorgos

The only way to reach Amorgos is by boat! You can catch one either from Piraeus, the port of Athens, or from nearby islands, such as Naxos, Paros and Santorini, among others.

If coming from abroad, you can either fly to Athens and then take a ferry from Piraeus, or fly to one of the islands mentioned above and catch a shorter ferry ride there. The journey from Athens typically takes 6 to 8 hours, depending on the boat and its itinerary. It might sound a bit long, but time on the boat flies and the destination is totally worth it!

If you are coming from Greece, you are Greek and shouldn't be reading this.

Amorgos has two ports: Aegiali and Katapola, which are approximately a 40-minute drive away from each other. Most ferries alternate daily between each port, and you can travel to either to best suit the day you want to arrive.

There are three companies that travel to Amorgos: Blue Star Ferries (which is the most common since it provides daily service), SeaJet, and Hellenic Seaways. All three companies provide service from Pireaus, which is the starting point, and then they reach other islands before arriving to Amorgos.

Lastly, there is also small ferry, called Skopelitis, that travels daily among the Small Cycladic Islands. It is usually a rough ride and we recommend this option only for the brave at sea!
